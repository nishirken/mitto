## Design Context

### Users
Personal daily driver on the Mudita Kompakt. Built by and for one person who uses this e-ink phone as their primary device and wants a Telegram client that feels native to the hardware.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, tactile, analog** — like a paper notebook or a typewritten letter. The interface should evoke nostalgia and intimacy, not cold efficiency. Every element should feel like it was placed by hand, not generated by a system.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary reference**: MuditaOS native apps. Mitto should feel like it shipped with the Kompakt — same visual language, same restraint, same sense of belonging to the device.
- **Tone**: Honest and unadorned. The beauty comes from precision and proportion, not decoration.
- **Theme**: Light only. Black on white, strict monochrome. This is an e-ink device — color doesn't exist here.
- **Typography**: Noto Sans (matching the device). Hierarchy through weight and size, not color.
- **Anti-references**: Flashy messaging apps (WhatsApp, Telegram's own client), anything with gradients, color accents, or rounded bubbly aesthetics that would feel alien on e-ink.

### Constraints
- 4.3" E Ink display, 800x480 @ 217 PPI (portrait: 480x800)
- Monochrome only — no color, no gradients
- No smooth animation — e-ink refreshes too slowly
- Touch-only, no cursor — generous tap targets (min 32px, prefer larger)
- High contrast: minimize gray usage, favor pure black on white for maximum legibility
- Whole pixel values only — fractional px rounds unpredictably on e-ink
- Font smoothing disabled — subpixel rendering creates artifacts on monochrome displays

### Design Principles
1. **Native to the device** — Every screen should feel like it was designed by Mudita's own team. Match the Kompakt's visual rhythm, not Telegram's.
2. **Ink on paper** — Think of the display as paper. Strong black type, generous whitespace, dotted and solid rules as the only ornamentation. If it wouldn't look right printed on a page, it doesn't belong.
3. **Touch with confidence** — Large, unambiguous tap targets with clear boundaries. The user should never wonder what's tappable or accidentally hit the wrong thing.
4. **Information density over decoration** — On a small screen, every pixel matters. Prioritize showing content over chrome. No element exists for purely decorative purposes.
5. **Warmth through craft** — The analog feeling comes from careful spacing, proportional type, and thoughtful layout — not from ornament. Like a well-typeset book.
